Class Highlights

Common belief in NFL circles is that it takes three years to determine the value of an NFL draft class. We've examined the 2006 draft class through their career end to determine the overall success of the 2006 draft class.


Depth in the Draft Class

Late round pick Marques Colston (252), from a I-AA Hofstra, reached a starting WR spot with the Saints. In his two seasons, he caught for more than 2,200 yards and 19 TDs.


Deuce Lutui was selected in the second round (41st overall) of the 2006 NFL draft by the Arizona Cardinals, where he is reunited with former USC teammate Matt Leinart. As a rookie, Lutui started 9 games. In 2007, he started 15 games. He went on to assist the Cardnals in the NFC West 9-7 record and blocked for Kurt Warner, seeing him to single season records in completions and TD passes.

First Round QBs

In his rookie year, Vince Young started 13 games for Tennessee, finishing with a 8-5 record. That season, he displayed his dual-threat ability, helping the Titans to multiple fourth-quarter comebacks and was named the NFL Offensive Rookie of the Year.


In 11 starts his rookie season, Matt Leinart threw for 2,547 yards, 11 touchdowns, 12 interceptions, and finished the season with a 4–7 record. That year he also set an NFL rookie record with 405 passing yards.

The Best of College Football

Mario Williams – He was an intregal part of the defense build for the Houston Texans. During his 3 seasons at North Carolina State; he finished with 25.5 sacks. Which was a school record until last year when it was broken by Bradley Chubb. It took Chubb 4 season to beat out Williams by half a sack.


Reggie Bush – He was the starting running back at USC for his freshman-junior seasons. He even won the 2005 Heisman Trophy. Which later on he had to vacate due to scandal at USC. Bush did end up having 3,169 rushing yards with 25 touchdowns and 1,301 receiving yards with 13 touchdowns.
